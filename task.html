FACTS ABOUT SPAGHETTI

  1. ORIGINS

    2. Spaghetti is originated in italy, specifically in the city of Naples,and its name coms from the italian word spaghetto, meaning little strings


  2. TYPES

    3. There are many types of spaghetti,including spaghetti alla, spaghetti integrale and spaghetti aglio e olio


  3. COOKING

    4. Spaghetti is typiccally cooked al dente, which means it still has a bit of bite or chew to it. Overcooking can make it mushy and unappetizing


  4. SAUCES

    5. Spaghetti is often paired with a variety of sauces, such as tomato-based marinara,creamy carbonara, or savory bolognese


  5. EATING

    6. In italy, its customary to twirl spaggetti around a fork, rather than cutting it with a knife


  6. WORLD RECORDS

    7. The longest spaghetti strand ever recorded was over 1,800 feet long!


  7. NUTRITION

    8. Spaghetti is a good source of carbohydrates and can be a healthy part of a balanced diet when paired with nutrient-rich sauces and toppings


SPAGHETTI INGREDIENTS

  • 250g spaghetti
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound grounded beef or alternative protein
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • salt and pepper
  • Grated parmesan

SPAGHETTI PREPARATIONS STEPS

  1. Boil water

    2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente


  2. Prepare sauce

    3. Heat olive oil in a pan, saute chopped onion and minced garlic until softened


  3. Add protein

    4. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks


  4. Add tomatoes and herbs

    5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, dried basil, and oregano. Season with salt and pepper.


  5. Simmer sauce

    6. Let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.


  6. Combine

    7. Drain cooked spaghetti and combine with the prepared sauce.


  7. Serve

    8. Top with grated parmesan cheese and enjoy
